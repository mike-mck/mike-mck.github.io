Michael McKelligott

I am a PhD student at the University of Chicago Harris School of Public Policy. I consider myself an applied microeconomist who investigates questions at the intersection of public policy and economics, often in the context of late nineteenth/early twentieth century America.

I am especially fascinated by the U.S. mortality transition and the incredible efforts made to collect and preserve detailed data that continues to provide valuable insights today.

About My Work

My current research explores the spread of public health knowledge, the impact of local information access on policy decisions, and the long-term effects of hygiene campaigns. I also examine how newspaper circulation affected public knowledge of Germ Theory and shaped local responses.
